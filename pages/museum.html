SAN AGUSTIN MUSEUM

The San Agustin Museum, located within the historic San Agustin Church in Manila, is a treasure trove of Filipino heritage and religious history. As part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site of the Baroque Churches of the Philippines, the museum offers visitors a unique glimpse into the country’s colonial past and its rich cultural legacy. Housed in the former convent of the San Agustin Church, the museum features an extensive collection of religious artifacts, artworks, and historical objects dating back to the Spanish colonial period.

BENCAB MUSEUM

The BenCab Museum, located in the scenic hills of Tuba, Benguet, just outside Baguio City, is a vibrant celebration of Philippine art, culture, and nature. Founded by renowned Filipino artist Benedicto "BenCab" Cabrera, the museum houses a stunning collection of contemporary and indigenous art, showcasing both the works of BenCab himself and other prominent Filipino artists. The museum is set amidst beautiful landscapes, with panoramic views of lush greenery and the iconic mountains of the Cordillera region.

NATIONAL MUSEUM OF FINE ARTS

The Fine Arts Museum in Manila, officially known as the National Museum of Fine Arts, is a cultural gem located in the heart of the Philippines' capital. Housed in a neoclassical building, it serves as a showcase for the rich history and diverse artistic heritage of the country. Established as part of the National Museum complex, this museum features an extensive collection of works from Philippine masters, including iconic pieces from renowned artists such as Juan Luna, Felix Resurreccion Hidalgo, and Fernando Amorsolo.

LAS CASAS FILIPINAS DE ACUZAR

Las Casas Filipinas de Acuzar, located in Bagac, Bataan, is a captivating heritage resort that transports visitors back in time to the Philippines' colonial past. Spanning over 400 hectares, this remarkable property is a living museum and cultural landmark that showcases the country's rich history through its meticulously restored Spanish-era houses. The resort features an impressive collection of ancestral homes that were carefully dismantled from various parts of the Philippines and reconstructed at Las Casas. These historic houses, with their distinct architecture, transport guests to the late 19th and early 20th centuries, allowing them to experience the grandeur and charm of a bygone era.

PINTO ART MUSEUM

Pinto Art Museum, located in Antipolo City, Philippines, is a contemporary art museum known for its expansive collection of Filipino art. Nestled within lush gardens, the museum features Mediterranean-inspired architecture with whitewashed walls, open courtyards, and rustic pathways. The galleries showcase a diverse range of artworks, including paintings, sculptures, and installations by prominent and emerging Filipino artists. The museum also offers panoramic views of Metro Manila and has a café where visitors can relax and enjoy the serene environment. The blend of art, nature, and architecture makes Pinto Art Museum a popular destination for art enthusiasts and tourists alike.
