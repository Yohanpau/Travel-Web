BORACAY

This is Boracay Island, a jewel of the Philippines and a destination that enchants travelers from around the world. Whether you’re in search of vibrant adventure, serene relaxation, or a mix of both, Boracay Island offers an experience like no other. In this guide, you’ll uncover the best time to visit, travel tips, the top things to do, where to eat, and the ideal places to stay on Boracay Island. SIARGAO

Siargao is a tropical paradise located in the province of Surigao del Norte, Philippines. Famous for its world-class surf breaks, pristine white-sand beaches, and laid-back island vibe, Siargao attracts adventure seekers and nature lovers alike. The island is home to Cloud 9, one of the most renowned surfing spots globally, drawing surfers from all over the world. Beyond surfing, Siargao boasts breathtaking destinations such as Sugba Lagoon, Magpupungko Rock Pools, and the stunning Naked, Daku, and Guyam Islands—perfect for island hopping and snorkeling. The lush coconut palm landscapes and scenic roads make exploring the island a unique experience, whether by motorcycle or boat. With a thriving food scene, vibrant nightlife, and warm, welcoming locals, Siargao offers a perfect mix of relaxation and adventure. Whether you're chasing waves, exploring lagoons, or simply unwinding by the beach, Siargao is an unforgettable destination.h

CORON PALAWAN

A breathtaking tropical escape known for its crystal-clear lagoons, towering limestone cliffs, and world-famous dive sites. This island paradise offers some of the most stunning landscapes and marine biodiversity in the country, making it a dream destination for travelers seeking both adventure and relaxation. One of Coron’s highlights is Kayangan Lake, often dubbed the cleanest lake in the Philippines, with its mesmerizing emerald waters surrounded by dramatic rock formations. Another must-visit is Twin Lagoon, where warm and cool waters meet, creating a unique swimming experience. For beach lovers, Malcapuya, Banana, and Bulog Dos Islands offer powdery white sand and crystal-clear waters perfect for sunbathing and snorkeling. Diving enthusiasts flock to Coron to explore its Japanese shipwrecks from World War II, considered one of the best wreck diving sites in the world. Snorkeling in Siete Pecados Marine Park also offers an unforgettable underwater experience with vibrant coral reefs and diverse marine life.

PUERTO PRINCESA UNDERGROUND RIVER

Puerto Princesa Underground River, located in Palawan, Philippines, is one of the New 7 Wonders of Nature and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. This breathtaking natural attraction is an 8.2-kilometer-long subterranean river that winds through a stunning limestone cave system before flowing directly into the South China Sea. Visitors can explore the first 4.3 kilometers of the river via a guided boat tour, witnessing impressive stalactites, stalagmites, and rock formations that have been shaped over millions of years. The cave is also home to a rich ecosystem, with thousands of bats, swiftlets, and unique marine species inhabiting the area. Aside from the underground river, the surrounding Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park offers lush forests, exotic wildlife, and beautiful coastal landscapes. Monkeys, monitor lizards, and hornbills are commonly spotted in the park, making it a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers.

AMANPULO LUXURY BEACH RESORT

Amanpulo Luxury Beach Resort is located in Pamalican Island, Palawan Philippines. It's best for luxury travelers, honeymooners and wellness retreats. Amanpulo is an ultra-luxurious private island resort offering unparalleled exclusivity, stunning white sand beaches, and world-class service. Accessible only by private charter flight, this hidden paradise is perfect for those seeking tranquility and seclusion.Amanpulo is a once-in-a-lifetime tropical getaway, offering the ultimate in luxury, privacy, and relaxation.
